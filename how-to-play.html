How to play

Build a continuous snake of words across the board. Longer words and clever crossings score more.

Basic rules

  1. Choose an empty edge tile to start.
  2. Enter a valid word. The first letter is placed on the current head tile, so you type the remaining letters.
  3. The next word must begin with the last letter of the previous word.
  4. Select the next tile on the board to choose the direction of movement.
  5. Existing tiles may be crossed only when the letter on the board matches the letter in the word.
  6. A word that has already been used in the same game cannot be used again.

Scoring

Word length is the base score. A two-letter word is worth 2 points, a three-letter word is worth 4 points, a four-letter word is worth 6 points, and longer words continue to increase by 2 points per added letter. Newly filled tiles add extra points, and crossings add extra points because they use the board more efficiently.

Snake mode

Snake mode places random usable letters on the board as apple markers. Apple tiles are not adjacent to one another when the board is created. If your path passes through an apple tile with the matching letter, you collect it. The apple bonus grows during the game: the first collected apple gives 10 points, the second gives 20 points, the third gives 30 points, and so on.

Language-specific notes

  • English allows normal words and filters many abbreviations or title-only entries.
  • Korean applies word-chain reading rules and focuses on noun-style playable entries.
  • Japanese uses hiragana input and blocks words ending in ん, following common shiritori rules.
