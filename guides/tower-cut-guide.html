Tower Cut guide

Tower Cut is a fast stack puzzle about comparing a broken tower with a target, removing wrong layers, and rebuilding the tower before time runs out.

Read the target first

Before moving pieces, compare the top, middle, and bottom sections of the tower. The target tells you what the final order should be. If a layer is already correct and supporting the right shape above it, protect it. If a layer is wrong near the bottom, fixing it early prevents repeated repairs later.

Cut with a purpose

A cut is strongest when it removes a clear mistake and exposes a useful rebuild point. Cutting randomly can make the tower shorter, but it also creates extra work. Look for the first layer where the current tower stops matching the target. That layer is usually the best place to act.

Rebuild in stable chunks

After a cut, rebuild from stable sections instead of chasing the entire target at once. Place blocks that lock in the bottom or a recognizable middle pattern. A stable chunk reduces future uncertainty and makes the remaining mismatch easier to see under time pressure.

Scoring consistency

Fast solves are valuable, but consistent scoring comes from avoiding wasted actions. A player who takes one extra second to make the right cut often beats a player who makes several rushed corrections. Perfect or near-perfect clears depend on reading the tower before the first move.

Practice pattern recognition

Most Tower Cut mistakes come from treating every board as completely new. Instead, watch for repeated shapes: swapped middle layers, extra top blocks, missing supports, and wrong bottom colors. Once those patterns become familiar, the correct cut is easier to choose.
