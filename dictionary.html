Dictionary

The game uses transformed dictionary data for playability, not a complete copy of every source dictionary.

Source approach

Korean, English, and Japanese word lists are prepared from public dictionary and lexical resources, then filtered for gameplay. The filters remove entries that would make the game feel unfair or noisy, such as one-letter Korean entries, many inflected Korean forms, many English abbreviations that are not ordinary words, and Japanese words that violate the ん-ending rule.

Because word-chain games are sensitive to missing common words, the game includes a suggestion flow. Suggested words are not accepted immediately. They are reviewed before being added to the accepted dictionary so duplicate, malformed, or unsuitable entries do not appear in play.

Why accepted and blocked lists both exist

Some entries are excluded for clear gameplay reasons. Keeping a separate blocked list lets the game explain why a word was rejected when possible. For example, an English entry may be blocked as an abbreviation while another word with the same spelling can remain accepted if it is also a normal word.

Notices

Dictionary data notices and license notes are maintained with the project. The game interface, scoring, board logic, ranking integration, and page design are original site work, while lexical resources are credited according to their source requirements.

If you think a common word is missing or incorrectly blocked, use the in-game suggestion button after an invalid-word result.
